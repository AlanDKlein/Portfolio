Education

I completed the Front End Web Development nanodegree program from Udacity© in October, 2020. This program consisted of lessons and hands-on projects in HTML5, CSS, and Javascript. Links to the various projects and the code I created for them are below in the "Projects" section of this page.

During my tenure at AT&T I earned a Masters Degree in Computer Science from Webster University in 2000. I earned this degree while working full time as a developer at AT&T.

In 1991 I completed the "Computer Career Program" at Maryville University. This was an accelerated 30-week program in computer programming and system design. This is the program that launched my career in the IT world.

My undergraduate work was completed in 1984 with a Bachelor of Health Science degree from the University of Missouri - Columbia.

Work Experience

My career in the IT industry began in 1991 when I completed the 30-week "Computer Career Program" at Maryville University. This program was an acclerated program designed to jump start a career in IT.
My first IT job was as a COBOL developer for a very small firm (less than 10 employees) and from there I moved to the corporate world with MCI Communications. Moving on to Bunge North America I began developing user interfaces using the PowerBuilder development tool. In 1996 because of my PowerBuilder experience I was hired by AT&T (Southwestern Bell at the time) where I spent 23 yearsT, first as a developer and the last 15 years as a technical architect.

I am a firm believer in life-long learning, and I've continued that pholosophy with the completion of Udacity's Front End Web Developer nanodegree program. The work that I've done from this program is listed under the "Projects" section of this page. There I have links to the code on GitHub for your reveiw there.
